Question 4

What patterns are emerging — by reason, by program type?

1

"Product-Market Fit Mismatch / Oversold" dominates — $7.3M across 4 accounts (52%)

The #1 attrition driver is customers who were sold more than they needed. Under Armour, YETI, Tapestry, and Vista Outdoor all reflect over-commitment on capacity or features. This is a sales-side issue, not a product gap.

2

Off-cycle ratio unusually high — 33% vs MC's 7%

6 of 18 accounts ($4.8M) are off-cycle events: restructures, bad debts, and ramp-downs. This is 4.7x the MC proportion, suggesting more mid-contract adjustments in the Commerce Cloud portfolio.

3

Dead ratio is 56% (10 of 18) — higher than MC's 45%

More than half of all attrition events are full logo losses. Once Commerce Cloud customers decide to leave, they leave entirely — there's less partial retention than Marketing Cloud.

4

Shopify is the only named competitor — $1.65M (1 account)

Cole Haan migrated to Shopify, citing CC not keeping pace with platform needs. Personalization pricing was the tipping point. No other competitor named in any account notes — this is not a competitive loss quarter.
