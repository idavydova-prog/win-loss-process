What patterns are emerging?

1
Financial & Contractual dominates — 9 accounts, $13.0M (69%)
Low Perceived ROI is the leading reason detail (Penney $5.24M, Sunrun $3.11M, GNC $1.98M, Raley's $400K). Combined with pricing dissatisfaction (Turntide $387K) and Limited Life Licenses (Zoro $275K), financial drivers represent the overwhelming majority of MC attrition this month.
2
Competitive displacement accelerating — 4 accounts, $6.86M
Attentive (Penney $5.24M — email/SMS), Klaviyo (Greenworks $368K, Scotts/Datorama $239K), and in-house solution (Lowe's $1.18M). The pattern is clear: ESP/CDP competitors are winning on perceived value, especially in RCG segment where brand marketers evaluate alternatives aggressively.
3
RCG segment bears heaviest burden — 8 accounts, $11.85M (63%)
RCG accounts represent the bulk of MC attrition. Penney ($5.24M), GNC ($1.98M), Kroger ($1.64M), Lowe's ($1.18M), and SharkNinja ($1.07M) are all RCG. Enterprise retail and CPG customers are actively rationalizing their MC spend.
4
Adoption issues: 5 accounts using "lack of expertise/sponsorship" as reason
Scotts x3 ($954K combined — internal enablement/sponsorship), Marvin Windows ($224K — lack of expertise), LAV US ($111K — expertise). These are potentially saveable with CSM intervention, adoption programs, or training investments.
