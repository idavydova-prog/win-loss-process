Question 4

What patterns are emerging — by reason, by program type?

1

"Low Perceived ROI" dominates — $7.3M across 9 accounts (44% of total)

The #1 attrition reason isn't competitors or bankruptcy — it's customers who don't see the value. These are the most saveable accounts because the product works, they just need help realizing ROI. Classic #ARI/#ADOPT territory.

2

Competitors are NOT the primary threat — only 14% of attrition

Only 4 of 28 accounts mention a competitor. Klaviyo (2 accounts, $941K), Braze (1, $1.2M), Fishbowl (1, $954K). The dominant story is internal execution, not market pressure.

3

Dead ratio is alarmingly high at 45% (11 of 28)

Nearly half of all attrition events are full logo losses — the customer is gone entirely. This suggests intervention is happening too late (or not at all). Early Red Account activation is critical.
