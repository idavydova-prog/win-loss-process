Methodology

Data Source

SOQL query against Org62 Opportunity object. Filters: Targeted_Clouds__c INCLUDES ('Core'), CloseDate May 1 – Jul 31 2026, Forecasted_Attrition__c ≤ -100000, StageName IN ('05 Closed', 'Dead Attrition'), Account.CSG_Region__c = 'AMER PACE'. Total records returned: 28.

Stage Methodology

This report includes BOTH “05 Closed” (12 records) and “Dead Attrition” (16 records) stages. Dead Attrition records represent confirmed full losses where the opportunity was terminated without renewal. 05 Closed records represent completed renewal events (which may show partial retention or full renewal).

Classification Framework

StepQuestionLogic
1Risk?Forecasted Attrition > $0 → Yes (all qualify)
2Actionable?Off-cycle forecasting, Bad Debt Hold, or CSM-confirmed non-actionable = Not Actionable
3OutcomeWin (Rate 100% + ACV = full risk amount, attrition averted) | Logo Loss (Dead Attrition or 05 Closed with 0% rate/$0 ACV) | Partial Save (some ACV retained, some attrited)

Win Classification Logic

A “Win” is defined as an attrition risk that was fully averted. The record appears in our dataset because Forecasted_Attrition was ≤ -$100K (indicating the risk existed), but the renewal closed at 100% rate with ACV equal to the at-risk amount — meaning the customer retained their full contract value. The forecasted attrition did not materialize. In Q2 Core, both FPL records qualify: the $798K and $194K risks were managed through a 30-day extension and the customer renewed in full.

Cross-Cloud Records

6 records include multiple clouds: 84.51 LLC (Core/Pardot/Industry), Rich Products (Core/MC), Niantic (Core/Slack), Stanley Black & Decker (Core/Slack), CSI Compressco (Core/Pardot), 7-Eleven (Core/Own). These may also appear in other cloud reports. Attrition is the total opportunity amount, not split by cloud.
